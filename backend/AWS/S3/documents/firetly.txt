Document Context: Definitions and Guidelines for Senior Fitness
Clinical Frailty Scale (Rockwood)

1 (Very Fit): Robust, active, and energetic. Exercises regularly.

2 (Well): Active, but less fit than category 1. May exercise occasionally.

3 (Managing Well): Medical problems are controlled, but not regularly active beyond routine walking.

4 (Vulnerable): Symptoms limit activities; often feel "slowed up" or tired. Not dependent on others for help.

5 (Mildly Frail): Needs help with high-order activities like shopping, heavy housework, or managing finances.

6 (Moderately Frail): Needs help with all outdoor activities and housework. May have problems with stairs and need minimal assistance with bathing or dressing.

7 (Severely Frail): Completely dependent on others for personal care.

Exercise Progression and Modification Guidelines

Start Slow: Always begin with the shortest duration and lowest intensity.

Listen to Your Body: Stop immediately if you feel pain, dizziness, or chest discomfort.

Adapt to Frailty: For individuals classified as Moderately to Severely Frail (5-7 on the scale), a healthcare professional should be consulted before starting. Chair-based and seated exercises are often a safer and more effective starting point.
