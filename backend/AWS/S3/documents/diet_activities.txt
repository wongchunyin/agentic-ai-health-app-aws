1. Protein-Rich Foods
Description: Eating adequate protein is crucial for maintaining muscle mass, which naturally declines with age.
Examples: Lean meats (poultry, fish), eggs, beans, lentils, nuts, and dairy products.
Amount: Experts recommend aiming for 25-30 grams of protein per meal.
Benefits: Helps maintain strength, reduces the risk of falls, and aids in repairing body tissues.

2. Fruits and Vegetables
Description: A wide variety of fruits and vegetables provide essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber.
Examples: Dark leafy greens (kale, spinach), cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cabbage), and colorful berries.
Amount: Aim for a mix of fruits and vegetables at every meal.
Benefits: Rich in antioxidants to protect cells, and fiber to support digestive health.

3. Whole Grains
Description: Choosing whole grains over refined grains provides more fiber and nutrients.
Examples: Whole-wheat bread, brown rice, oatmeal, and quinoa.
Amount: Should be a part of most meals to provide sustained energy.
Benefits: Helps regulate blood sugar, lowers cholesterol, and supports bowel regularity.

4. Healthy Fats
Description: Incorporating healthy fats into the diet is important for brain health and heart health.
Examples: Avocados, nuts, seeds, and oily fish like salmon, herring, or mackerel.
Amount: A small handful of nuts or a serving of oily fish a few times a week is recommended.
Benefits: Provides omega-3 fatty acids that are linked to better cognitive function and reduced inflammation.

5. Staying Hydrated
Description: Drinking plenty of fluids throughout the day is essential, as the sense of thirst can decrease with age.
Examples: Water, low-fat milk, herbal teas, or soups.
Amount: Aim for 6-8 glasses of fluid per day.
Benefits: Prevents dehydration, which can lead to fatigue and confusion, and helps with nutrient absorption and digestion.s
